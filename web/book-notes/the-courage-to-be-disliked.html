The Courage To Be Disliked Notes

Satvik Gupta

Key Takeaways/ Notes

  • Traditional Psychology views events as Cause-Emotion-Effect. Waiter spilled coffee over me, I got angry, and I shouted on him. This is termed Aetiology . Teleology, or Adlerian psychology, takes a different approach. It follows Cause-Goal-Emotion. The waiter spilled coffee over me. In my mind, I formed a goal to make the waiter regret his mistake. I made myself angry because anger was the best emotion to achieve that goal.
  • We are one. We are not separate from our mind, we are not separate from our emotions. We are a unified whole. We cannot say “The emotion made me do it.” The emotion is part of us.
  • The past is the past. Trauma is in the past. It does not matter. If the past created our personalities, the world would be reduced to determinism. Traumatic experiences have influence, but it is upto us what that influence is. We make our life what it is.
  • The world isn’t complicated. We find it complicated because we make it seem complicated.
  • Sometimes we may choose unhappiness. “I am happy when I’m unhappy”. Because despite the inconveniences, we judge our current lifestyle/personality to be the best,easiest and most practical way to handle the world. It’s like being stuck in a local minima and unable to find the global (or even a better local). But this is a good thing. Because if we chose our lifestyle, we can choose it all over again. Anyone can decide at any moment to change. All we have to do is to power through our brain’s resistance to it.
  • You need the courage to be happy.
  • We enter this world as helpless beings. All humans strive to move forward from this state of helplessness. This is called pursuit of superiority. We always want to better ourself. Feelings of inferiority are also useful in bettering ourself. But, prolonged feelings of inferiority may form an inferiority complex, which is bad.
    • Feeling of inferiority - I’m not well educated, so I need to work harder than others.
    • Inferiority Complex - I’m not well educated, so I can’t get a good job and have a good life.
    • Also related to superiority complex, a fabricated sense of superiority. No man who must say I am the King is a true king
  • Think of people as your comrades. Don’t consider them competition.
  • Anger is a tool used for achieving a goal. That goal can be realized without anger too.
  • Separate tasks. Some tasks are yours, some belong to other people. Don’t intrude on other people’s tasks. Don’t try to do their tasks for them. Help them, if they ask. Let them know you are available for help, but unless they ask, don’t intrude.
  • Live your life the best way you know how. What judgement other people pass on it is their task.
  • A stone on a hill rolls down. It cannot stop itself. It gives into the inclination. But we are humans, we are capable of resisting inclination.
  • Not wanting to be disliked is a natural desire (an inclination), but we must resist it. If we live our life in a way that people won’t dislike us, we will be living life according to them. If we aren’t living life for ourself, then who is living for us? Freedom is being disliked by other people
    • I don’t want to be disliked, but I don’t mind if I am
    • Not wanting to be disliked is my task. Whether a person dislikes me or not is their task.
  • We have a sense of worth when we feel we can contribute to the community.
  • Contribute to others doesn’t mean self-sacrifice.
  • Happiness is the feeling of contribution.
  • Live the moments. If your goal is to get to the top of the mountain top, you can take a helicopter ride there, stay for 5 minutes, and helicopter back. If you don’t make it to the top, you’ve failed. Instead, if your goal is to simply climb the mountain, you won’t fail. And you can enjoy and grow from the climb.
  • Whatever meaning life has is given it to it by the individual.
